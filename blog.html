Camel feeding

The nutritional needs of camels are low compared to other animals, as camels are distinguished by their high efficiency in benefiting from food, especially from fodder that is poor in its nutritional content. The animal suffices a quantity of food within the range of 1.25% of the body weight to cover the preservative nutritional needs, which is equivalent to about 12.5 grams of ingested dry matter per kilogram of body weight. It must be taken into consideration that this amount should be increased by 25% when the camels go out to graze, in exchange for the effort exerted by the animals. during grazing. During pregnancy, nutritional needs must be increased by 20% during the first 8 months, and the increase reaches 50% during the last 4 months of pregnancy, between which it is taken into account that camels obtain twice their preservative needs during the first 6 months of milk. Good and decreases to one and a half times the preservative needs during the second 6 months. The quantities eaten from natural pastures depend on the type of available vegetation, the percentage of moisture, the degree of palatability, and the daily grazing hours counter. It was found that the amount of dry matter eaten from good pasture for one animal is about 6 kg per day, up to about 12 kg in the case of high predominance of juicy plant species. Taste, and decreases to about 1.5 kg in the case of the dominance of saline and thorny plant species. The camel has gained its reputation as the ship of the desert since ancient times due to its superior ability to withstand thirst for long periods. The length of the thirst endurance period depends on the nutritional content of pasture plants, the percentage of moisture in them, the concentration of salts in drinking water, the season of the year, and the physiological condition of the camel (pregnancy - birth - milk - growth). And camels have the ability to drink water in large quantities and at a very fast rate. One animal can drink 10-15 liters of water per minute, and it can also drink water equivalent to a third of its weight after long periods of thirst. Camels have the ability to drink salty water with a concentration that may reach 20,000 parts per million, which is equivalent to more than half of the salinity concentration in sea water.
